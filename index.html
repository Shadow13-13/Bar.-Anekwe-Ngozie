Biography

A career built on clarity and commitment.

Bar. Anekwe Ngozie is one of Onitsha's most respected legal practitioners — a lawyer who combines deep statutory knowledge with a practical understanding of the realities her clients face every day. With over 15 years of active practice, she has become a trusted counsel to businesses, families, institutions, and individuals across Anambra State and Southeast Nigeria.

She attended the Nigerian Law School and was called to the Nigerian Bar, thereafter establishing a practice grounded in the conviction that legal services should be accessible, clear, and genuinely useful. Her early years sharpened her skills in commercial and civil matters — a foundation she has since expanded across all major branches of Nigerian law.

Her approach is marked by precision: careful reading of facts, rigorous legal research, and communication that cuts through complexity. Clients consistently describe working with her as transformative — not simply because she wins cases, but because they leave understanding exactly what happened and why.

"The law exists to serve people — not to intimidate them. My work is to make that service real, practical, and dignified for every client who walks through my door."

Experience & Achievements

A track record across many disciplines.

Over her 15+ year career, Bar. Anekwe Ngozie has handled matters ranging from complex corporate mergers and CAC regulatory compliance, to bitterly contested family inheritance disputes and land title litigation under the Land Use Act. She has appeared before the High Court, Court of Appeal, and various tribunals and arbitration panels.

She has advised numerous Onitsha-based businesses — from sole proprietorships navigating their first CAC registration to established conglomerates managing multi-party contracts. Her network of vetted professional collaborators means that even when a matter demands specialist input, clients always receive seamless, coordinated service.

Beyond individual practice, she is a regular contributor to legal education and public awareness initiatives within the NBA Onitsha Branch, and has spoken on issues of women's rights, property law, and business compliance at local fora.

Philosophy

The values behind the practice.

Bar. Anekwe practices within a clear ethical framework shaped by the Nigerian Bar Association's Rules of Professional Conduct (RPC 2023). She does not promise outcomes; she promises diligence. She does not take on cases she cannot serve well; she refers where she should and collaborates where she must.

Her philosophy is straightforward: every client deserves to understand their legal position, every matter deserves thorough preparation, and justice — however imperfect the system — is worth fighting for thoughtfully.

Practice Areas

Comprehensive legal representation across all major branches of Nigerian law — each handled with individual attention and 15+ years of expertise.

⚖️

Corporate & Commercial Law

Business formation, CAC registration, shareholder agreements, mergers & acquisitions, regulatory compliance under CAMA, IP counselling for startups, and corporate governance advisory for growing enterprises in Anambra and beyond.

🏛️

Civil Law & Litigation

Contract disputes, debt recovery, negligence and tort claims, injunctions, declaratory orders, and full court representation before the High Court and Court of Appeal. We handle both plaintiff and defendant mandates with equal rigour.

🔖

Constitutional Law

Human rights enforcement under the 1999 Constitution (as amended), fundamental rights applications, judicial reviews, and petitions before constitutional and superior courts across Nigeria.

⚔️

Criminal Law & Defence

Bail applications, defence representation in all criminal matters from the Magistrate Court to the Court of Appeal, appeal filings, and rights advocacy to ensure every accused person receives a fair defence under Nigerian law.

🏠

Property & Real Estate Law

Land acquisition and title verification, lease agreements, purchase and sale transactions, mortgage advisory, and property dispute resolution under the Land Use Act. Deep familiarity with Anambra State land registry processes.

👨‍👩‍👧

Family Law

Divorce and separation proceedings, child custody and access, spousal maintenance, adoption processes, and matrimonial property — handled with sensitivity and strict confidentiality under both statutory and customary frameworks.

📜

Estate Planning & Succession

Will drafting and execution, intestate succession advisory, estate administration, trust structures, and probate proceedings — ensuring your assets are protected and your wishes respected.

👷

Labour & Employment Law

Employment contract review, wrongful dismissal claims, workplace rights enforcement, industrial dispute resolution, and compliance advisory under Nigeria's Labour Act — for both employers and employees.

💡

Intellectual Property Law

Trademark registration, copyright advisory, patent counselling, and IP enforcement through the Nigerian Copyright Commission and related bodies — protecting the creative and commercial assets of individuals and businesses.

🧾

Taxation Law

Tax compliance advisory, FIRS dispute representation, tax structuring for businesses, and guidance on state and federal tax obligations — including the latest updates from the Finance Acts.

⛽

Oil & Gas Law

Regulatory compliance, upstream and downstream contract advisory, joint venture agreements, and dispute resolution in Nigeria's energy sector — with reference to the Petroleum Industry Act (PIA) 2021.

🚢

Maritime & Admiralty Law

Shipping contracts, port operations advisory, cargo dispute resolution, and international trade legal support — particularly relevant for Onitsha's significant river commerce and import/export businesses.

✊

Human Rights & Public Interest Law

Advocacy for fundamental rights violations, representation for individuals and groups whose constitutional rights have been infringed, and collaboration with NGOs and civil society organisations on public interest litigation. Justice is not a privilege — it is a right.
